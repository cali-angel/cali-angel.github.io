Lecture - SEO for ecommerce Part 1, 2

This assignment inclulded a lecture from Lynda.com about how to make good SEO's which means search engine opimization. It's mainly for Ecommerce puposes, but most of it was about making modifications on the pages html code themselves. These are my notes from that lecture.

Overview

  • What is SEO  for ecommerce?
  • SEO - the process of assessing the current on and off page factors of a website. Also, improving these factors to boost rankings in a search engine.
  • Optimization - an act, process, or methodology of making something as fully perfect, functional, or effective as possible.
  •   Includes keywords, meta description, headings, alt tags, inbound links, & page titles
  • This helps search engines get a better idea of what your online shop is about and this can help it when people search. It is also an ongoing process.
  • Focus on traffic, since this will help search engines when it comes to ranking your site
  • It all adds up to the total grade - ranking based on the result of many factors combined together to determine an overall grade.
  • On-page vs off-page SEO
  • Refer to the things you can do on your page to help get traffic, versus the things that rely on other things off your site that help get traffic.
  • Off page factors include social media, blog posts, and review sites
  • Off page factors can always be improved
  • Usability
  • Needs to be usable and appeal to all
  • Acquisition of users and retention of keeping the users on the site go hand in hand
  • Design and write for customers first, then for the search engines
  • Never sacrifice usability for rankings
  • Give vistors direct access of info
  • Focus on proper design techniques
  • The buying process
  • First the user recognizes they have a need
  • Then they explore the needs solution
  • Once they find it, they evaluate the options
  • When they find the right option, they buy
  • After, they evaluate the purchase in the form of the review
  • SEO is normally in the second step, but can be put in the first step or the 3rd.
  • Setting expectations
  • SEO results may shift up and down on a weekly basis
  • Its and ongoing process, results aren’t immediate
  • Make sure your site deserves to be the top spot
  • Target niches
  • Pay for advertisement for a short while and use it to your advantage
  •   Understanding exactly who your customer is
  • Look for trends with customers
  • Can use tools like surveys, pop up notifications, and contacting old customers for interviews
  • Creating buyer personas
  • Helps to determine who will buy from your shop
  • Making these personas help us how to design and market our site
  • Quality traffic = higher sales\
  • Using simple and long tail keyword searches
  • Most likely, the first 3 sites on a search will get the purchase.
  • Using simple keywords has high competition and high search volume. They are also very expensive
  • Long tail key words have low competition and the search volume is low. But they are cheaper and people who search for them are more willing to purchase
  • Getting the most out of keyword research
  • Brainstorm ideas
  • Use google’s suggestion box
  • Use google’s planner tool
  • Explore competition associated with keyword
  • Finalize your list
  • Quality over quantity
  • Carrying out competitor research
  • Search keywords and see the competition
  • Use SEMruch to indetify the majore players
  • Run side by side search of competitors
  •  Harnessing page titles and meta info
  • Titles are the most important since they are the first thing you see on a search
  • 50-60 charachters max
  • Use most important kewords
  • Use unique titles for each page of site
  • Its important to use good descriptions to show the user what your website is about.
  • They also have a max length
  • 150-160 characters long
  • Written in a clear and understandable way
  • Use unique descriptions for each page of the site
  • Exploring page structure: headings, copy, and images
  • Use headings that are structured
  • Use keywords in our page and body information
  • Use alt tags on images
  • Crafting internal links to help search engines explore your site
  • Search engines look through your page just as a human would. It follows all links and it ranks it like that.
  • Anchor are used to make links on text
  • Avoid using generic text like “click here”
  • Don’t use images as links without alt or at all
  •  Large footer areas are a great location for internal links
  • Use persistent navigation on your page
  • Earning inbound links to raise your ranking
  • External links are links that point from an outside site to your site.
  • They can help boost your rankings
  • The third party sites link to your site using relevant anchor text
  • Eliminate duplicate content to not get penalized
  • Use unique titles/descriptions for each page of website
  • Google may penalize you for this
  • If a drop shipper, don’t reuse manufacture description of a product
  • Use Canonical tags to refer to the prefered site
  • Avoiding unnatural keyword usage
  • Keyword density Formula: (targeted keywords / total words on page) x 100
  • Focus more on natural writing and then fix keyword density
  • Don’t use the cloaked text trick, which is when keywords are hidden from users, but the search engine sees them, which makes that site higher ranking in searches
  • Will be heavily penalized
  • Using rich media to promote your SEO results
  • Make sure that the media are not slowing down your sites speed.
  • The google algorithm will also see the file sizes and rank it accordingly
  • Compress your pictures before you put it on your website
  • Provide a summary about video context
  • Offer social media links to share, and to create link building
  • Youtube is a great place to host your video and embed them in the site. It will also play on all devices.
  • If you host your own vids, make sure your player is compatible with all devices
  • Minimize your website loading speeds
  • If your website speed is too slow, it will really reduce traffic to your site
  • We can use pagespeeed insight to see how fast our pages load. It also offers suggestions
  • Optimize all videos and images
  • Use quality hosts
  • Minify code (minifycode.com)
  • Use Content delivery networks. They will readily have your files in geospecific locations for it to load faster
  • Correlate your SEO efforts to sales or other quantitative measures
  • Use measurable goals
  • Examples are product purchases, newsletter signups, social interactions, and product reviews.
  • Try to figure out what keywords and searches led to purchases
  • Use google analytics to to make goals
  • Aligning your domain names with your target audience
  • Find a domain name that is related to your industry
  •  If your in a packed industry, try making up names or mixing words that relate to you industry
  • 3 words is a good range for your domain name
  • Using hyphens is good but be careful how much you use them, limit to one
  • Shorter names are better
  • Designing your landing page to driver deeper engagement
  • Make sure your homepage explains what your product will do for the customer and make it bold.
  • Make sure that it also leads to users going deeper in the site.
  • Keep content on your home page light and easy to skim
  • In a design standpoint, make sure to break up content into bite size chunks rather than long blocks of text.
  • Match the tone of your user within the design
  • Optimizing your product pages via breadcrumbs, pages titles, and social links
  • Breadcrumbs are links that show you where you are on the site
  • Use social sharing buttons
  • Make sure your titles are well structured
  • Start with a product name, then a category, and then a vertical line and the business name
  • Write unique product descriptions
  • Understanding how mobile usability affects SEO
  • Having a mobile friendly badge to indicate that your site is mobile accessible is good for the SEO
  • Run the google mobile friendly test tool
  • Avoid using flash or data-heavy media on mobile
  • Consider usability in your site design (fonts, buttons, etc.)
  • Avoid pop up windows and keeping in mind the limited screen size
  • Reduce the length of items to keep in mind the mobile versions
  • Using optimizely for A/B testing
  • Aka split testing
  • Optimizely is a program that allows you to make a different version of your site and then shows it to a portion of your traffic. You can make goals and experiments to see if one version is better than the other.
  • Harnessing google analytics to better understand site visitors
  • It tells us the number of visitors of the site, what pages they went to, and also where the countries the customer comes from
  • Tells us what search engines the customers used and even the screen resolutions of their devices. This can help us to narrow down on what customers we attract and will help when we want to make adjustments to the site
  • Shows the acquisition of our site.
  • Employing heatmapping tools to measure and monitor page usage
  • This can show what the users are clicking on and how far the users go down the site
  • Crazy egg is one of those heatmapping tools. It is for purchase but offers a free trial
  • It can help with design tweeks to help users have a better experience
  • Applying Moz for larger scale sites
  • Its higher priced
  • It is a suite of tools like data analytics, rankings, etc.
  • Choosing a product to sell
  • Ecommerce has become cheaper to start and the software has become easier to use
  • Choose a business model and then choose your platform
  • 1, choose an interest. 2, choose a product (physical, and virtual). If you choose physical, you need to think about the acquisition, storage, and fulfillment. Make your products yourself, or buy wholesale. Drop-shipping is a new fad, where you get the purchase and then ship the info to the manufacture. Another option is to move into manufacturing.
  • Virtual products include books, software, or premium service to a site.
  • Next step is to see who you want to sell your products to.
  • Defining your audience
  • Who are your customers?
  • What problems do they have?
  • How can you address those problems?
  • Create personas, which are representations of your customers.
  • Focus on one of your personas, since you will have a higher chance of connecting with that persona. Also make as much details to your personas as much as you can.
  • Organizing products
  • Bundles, add ons, configurable products, subscriptions.
  • Gravity forms has an all in one package, with a set price
  • Ninja forms are free to use, but cost with advanced settings
  • Having bundles are educational, inclusive, and offer a price break
  • Can also sell add-ons. They sell products that enhance others, save catalog space, and are an easy upsell.
  • You can offer a customizable products, which focuses on a single product and can also include add-ons.
  • Offer subscriptions. Convenience is a feature,and you can reward the customer.
  • Offering pre-sales to validate your product
  • Try selling one product before your actually start a dedicated store.
  • Can use forums, an in person interviews, and blogs
  • Crowdfunding is one good way to gauge interest in your product
  • If you don’t get pre-orders, it’s ok since it saves you time and you get feedback on your product.
  • You can always cancel the order
  • Really get feedback and get testimonials
  • Follow your intuition
  • Using marketing to increase site traffic
  • You either invest time or money, or both
  • Advertising shows quick results and there are many advertising options out there. There is a learning curve however to focus on target words. Also you have to have money to make it with advertising.
  • SEO is another way to attract customers
  • Content marketing: creating content to educate audience
  • Its slow to show results but its free.
  • Social media is also a great way to attract customers
  • Cost of acquisition: the investment needed to get someone to buy a product
  • No effort= no traffic
  • Understanding customers lifetime value(CLV)
  • Two main values: Acquisition cost, or customer lifetime value
  • CLV is an estimate of how much profit you will get from each customer.
  • Always try to lower your CoA and raise your CLV
  • CoA = cost per visit / conversion rate
  • Offering variety packs will encourage customers to buy more
  • Offering newsletters will remind customers to come back
  • You can entice them with similar products
  • Increasing CLV with repeat customers
  • High quality products = return customers without encouragement, higher R&D, production and branding, yet customers are loyal. Also advantage if product is needed regularly.
  • Consumable products have huge opportunity for repeat customers and have a higher CLV which means you can spend more on acquisition.
  • 3 ways of building relationships: social media, newsletters, marketing automation
  • Retaining customers is easier than finding new ones
  • Increasing your average order value
  • Have premium products. No price caps and try bundles that add value
  • Price anchor: a cognitive bias to rely too heavily on the first piece of info.
  • Cross selling: encouraging someone to buy a related product
  • Free shipping can entice customers. Calculate the average order value first, then set an unlockable free shipping above that.
  • Using tiered pricing can entice users as well to add a couple more dollars to their cart
  • Small increases in AOV can make a big difference
  • Getting paid
  • Typically need 4 things: a website, payment gateway, merchant account, and bank account.
  • Website is where the customers add products to cart and checkout. They enter their credit card info and that website sends it to the payment gateway.
  • Payment gateways check if funds are available. There are 2 types, traditional and modern.
  • Traditional gateways needs a merchant account which draws funds and deposits them into bank account
  • Modern gateways authorize and draw the funds and then deposits into bank account
  • Authorizing a charge: checking if funds are available.
  • Capture a charge: transfers funds
  • Capture only when you ship products
  • Authorizations last only a couple of days.
  • Examining your needs
  • Paypal is a non-credit card option. It's safer, but some don’t have accounts.
  • Wordpress has plug-ins for paypal
  • You  can also use a person’s amazon account to check out
  • Regional restrictions: some gateways only accept payments from one country in one currency.
  • They might charge exchange fees for the currencies not supported
  • Stripe is a good software to use
  • Credit cards = risk. If you don’t feel comfortable, then you can turn to an off site gateway.
  • 2 parts: 1. Enter billing info and shipping address on site. 2. Enter credit card info and authorize payment off site
  • Recurring payments may not be allowed on gateways. Or it may be difficult to integrate with off site gateways. They may also lack flexibility. Check to see if platforms support it as well.
  • Checking out with paypal
  • You use the paypal buttons but their functionality isn’t really great.
  • Building a site that integrates the cart with paypal is a good feature
  • Checking out with stripe
  • It is a good gateway to use for credit cards
  • Also has a test mode
  • Calculating shipping costs
  • There are lots of options for shipping
  • Basic shipping option is flat rate. Its available on most ecommerce platforms and is easy to set up. There is also no need to set up an account to ship your items.
  • Free shipping is good since people don’t like shipping fees. Expenditure based free shipping will likely cause the customer to buy more. Hide other shipping methods when free shipping is available
  • Estimates get a quote from mainstream shipping providers. Its precise in cost but more difficult to set up.
  • Most likely need to create an account which is free but takes time to set up. Need to take weight and dimensions for all items and box dimensions.
  • Ecommerce platforms pack the items for the estimate, but you have to figure out how to pack it later
  • Table of rates - easy to set up and test, uses weight, number of items, types of items etc. but it usually isn’t free
  • Managing orders
  • These ship the orders, send tracking info, refund the orders, etc.
  • Want to make sure they are easy to use
  • Can use wordpress plugins for orders. Also woocommerce can print the shipping labels.
  • Some platforms work with gateways to refund simultaneously, while others aren’t supported so you must go to the gateway and refund manually.
  • Managing inventory and listings across platforms
  • When using bigger sites its your product, their customers. Feedback is rare, contact info isn’t shared, but they have a large customer base. Need to manage the inventory and product data.
  • Lots of automation on this, but require a monthly fee
  • Understanding taxes
  • Always consult a tax professional
  • Nexus: a physical presence such as a storefront or a warehouse.
  • Inclusive: taxes are included in displayed product price
  • Exclusive: taxes are added to displayed product price
  • You can manually input taxes or get a service that does it for you.
  • Some items are tax exempt in the US
  • Collecting EU value-added tax (VAT)
  • Different countries in europe collect different taxes
  • Tax laws have many subtleties - use a platform that integrates with a tax service
  • Taxamo is a service that calculates EU VAT taxes for you.
  • Understanding the basics of secure sockets layer (SSL)
  • You can be financially liable if credit card info is stolen.
  • Unsecured traffic can be intercepted
  • All sensitive info should be entered on a secure page
  • Https: hypertext transfer protocol secure
  • SSL: secure sockets layer protocol
  • Be considerate of your customers information
  • Many hosts install ssl for a small fee
  • PCI DSS compliance: payment card industry data security standard for handling credit card numbers
  • Factors that are involved: ecommerce platform, hosting environment, and organizational practices
  • Protecting sensitive data
  • Just because SSL is installed doesn’t mean it’s always in use.
  • Configure SSL: use .htaccess, manage through platform, install a plugin
  • Protect the wordpress admin login page
  • Outline a security plan for your site
  • Using native wordpress platforms
  • Functionality: order management, multiple payment gateways, live shipping estimates, digital file delivery, etc.
  • These wordpress based platforms are open source and GPL certified
  • Follow the freemium platforms, the core functionality is free, but the advanced functionality is paid for.
  • Platform extensions are official, or unofficial
  • Ecommerce is incredibly complex - choose a developer over features
  • Implementing third-party platforms
  • Hosted platforms mean you don’t own or manage the code, it’s easier to set up, but it’s harder to customize
  • Test you site
  • Lots of moving parts in a ecommerce site, such as displaying product reviews, getting live shipping quotes, accepting payment, and securing your site
  • Checkout process is the most important part of your site.
  • Test separate cart sessions, shipping methods, each product, each payment gateway, and anything else.
  • Small errors cause major problems, check your info thoroughly.
  • Optimization
  • Lots to optimize on your site
  • Use analytical tools
  • Improvements: better photography, streamlining checkout, and reorganizing products.
  • Be detail oriented
  • Always make improvements
